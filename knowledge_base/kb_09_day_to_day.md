# KB-09: Day-to-Day Work

**Q: What does your typical week look like?**

In a given week, my time is roughly divided like this:

**Architecture & Technical Direction (~25%)**
System design reviews, platform evolution decisions, technical debt assessment, and capability planning. I'm deeply involved in how systems are built and where the architecture is heading — setting the technical direction for the team and ensuring design decisions hold up at scale.

**Delivery & Programme Management (~20%)**
Sprint ceremonies, roadmap tracking, dependency management, and risk surfacing. Keeping multiple workstreams in sync and moving against a plan — and flagging blockers before they become problems.

**Stakeholder Management (~15%)**
Regular alignment with business, compliance, and senior leadership — ALM, LTRA, and CIO-level conversations. Translating engineering progress into business language and managing expectations across the portfolio.

**AI/GenAI Initiatives (~10%)**
Driving AI adoption within the platform — anomaly detection in production, GenAI-generated insights for on-call teams, and identifying where automation can reduce manual toil. I stay connected to where the technology is moving, both inside work and through independent projects.

**Coding (~20%)**
I write production-level code — not just scripts or experiments. This includes platform features, pipeline logic, and engineering work that ships. Staying hands-on is a deliberate choice; it keeps my technical judgement sharp and my credibility with the team real.

**People & Team (~10%)**
1:1s, performance conversations, hiring interviews, and mentoring — woven through the week rather than a separate block. I've grown the team from 8 to 16+ and am currently mentoring 3 engineers toward Tech Lead roles. The team runs well when this is consistent, not occasional.
